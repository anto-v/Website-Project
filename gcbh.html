Kill count - The Site

A new addition to the Kill Count channel is the Golden Chainsaw/Dull Machete section, where James ranks the kills in terms of execution, and because we always agree with James A. Janisse (again, pls don't sue me), below are the best (golden chainsaw) and worst (dull machete) kills of every movie counted.

Leprechaun in the Hood (2000)

While none of the kills are really that impressive, this one at least got some good camera work and special effects (Mack's Bodyguard hole blown through chest by Lep magic)

This barely qualifies as a kill, the man just screams and that's kind of about it (Jackie Dee's ??? death)

Carrie (1976)

Ok, yeah you can see the wires in SOME frames, but this is such a symbolic kill it can't be overlooked (Carrie's mom death)

Absolutely underwhemling given the movie's other kills (Tommy Ross hit in head w/ bucket)

The Invisible Man (1933)

Absolutely unexpected, and totally cool visual effects even in today's standards (Train Crashed by Griffin)

Not necesarily a bad kill, especially considering the actor's performance, but it just sort of pales in comparison with the other kills (Chief Detective strangled & hit in head w/ stool)

Killer Klowns From Outer Space (1988)

The goofiest kill in the movie, great comic relief and practical effects (Big Top Burger Boy taken out to the trash by clown uppercut)

Only the worst because it's painstakingly irritating to try to count (The Cotton Candy Cocoons)

Final Destination (2000)

Truly, truly unexpected and it really set the tone for the kills in later movies (Billy Hitchcock 3/4 decapitated by some metal shrapnel)

I know FD death logic is a lot of Tom & Jerry trap-esque kills but this was way too big of a strech for the first movie (Mrs. Valerie Lewton glass in neck, knife in chest)
